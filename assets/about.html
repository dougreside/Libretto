About LIBRETTO

Libretto is a prototype of an ebook reader capable of reading and comparing texts that exist in multiple versions and which are part of a work that also important encompasses non-textual components (such as music). Developed by New York Public Library's Digital Curator for the Performing Arts, Doug Reside and funded by a generous grant from the National Endowment for the Humanities, this app suggests a possible future for critical editions of multimodal texts such as opera and musical theater

This working prototype is compiled from open source code available under an MIT License at:
https://github.com/dougreside/Libretto

This prototype is subscribed to a feed (generated from a Google Spreadsheet) which may occasionally update the library with additional titles that you may chose to download or ignore. By default, it includes a copy of a draft of a new variorum edition of The Black Crook, an 1866 melodrama that early popular theater historians have occasionally (and with little reason) called the "First American Musical."

How to use

  • Navigate through scenes by clicking the Next and Previous icons at the bottom of the page.
  • Navigate from the Introduction and through different versions of the text by swiping right or left. The text should automatically stay fairly closely synched.
  • When you encounter a speaker icon [ ], tap it to listen to the music associated with that moment in the text.
  • To change the audio associated with a moment, tap the eighth note icon while the music is playing and then tap "Select Audio"

Eventually, some titles released to the app may not bundle the music with the text (perhaps because an editor was able to obtain the rights to distribute the text but not a recording). When this is the case, you will be prompted to "Select Audio". You can then attach any music stored on your device.
