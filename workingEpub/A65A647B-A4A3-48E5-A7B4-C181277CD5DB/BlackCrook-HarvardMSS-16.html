Scene Five
The forest at night (in 1). Lights down.
Sound of a hunters horn, R.H., and very faint echo--Greppo enters R.H.
Greppo
What's the use--now what is the use? The oftener I call, the fainter comes the answer. Here it is quite dark, Master waiting for the horses and I wandering about like a jack o' lantern, I don't know where --following the horn of some goblin huntsman. There's some devil's work going on--I know there's some devil's work going on!
(Starting)
I beg his infernal Majesty's pardon, I hope I haven't said anything to offend, but strange sights and sounds are in the air, birds, that ought to have gone to roost an hour ago, fly screaming from tree to tree
*[on previous page] The dismal screech-owl from its hole in the oak answers to the croak of the dreary monk-raven
and just now I saw a big fat buck scampering through the forest with his tail on fire. Oh, dear, oh dear, what fearful omens. And I'm to be married tomorrow. If anything should happen to me, If my poor, dear, darling little Carline should happen to be left a widow before she's made a wife,--If I should never taste--that is, if I should never know--Oh, Lord, it won't bear thinking of. I'll try once more.
(winds the horn--echo very faint and distant, L.H.)
Worse and worse. Oh, Lord, Oh Lord! (Exit L.H.) I want to ho home
(Exit L.H.)
