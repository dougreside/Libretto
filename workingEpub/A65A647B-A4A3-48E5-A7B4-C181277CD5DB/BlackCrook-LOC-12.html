Act 4.
A lapse of six months.
Scene I.--An apartment in the castle of Wolfenstein. (In 1.)
[Music. Enter Barbara, R.H.]
Bar.
Alas that things ever fall so ill. 'Tis now six months since that never-to-be-forgotten night and still no news of the lost mistress of Wolfenstein ; nor yet of his lordship who has sworn an oath never to return to the castle until he has brought her back, and revenged himself on the horrible monster who has stolen her. And that graceless baggage, Carline, too, to go off at the same time, leaving all the cares of the household to fall on my poor shoulders ; and then to make the matter worse, spells and witchcraft turn all things upside down. The cows milk vinegar. The wells are dry. The hens lay haddled eggs.
Puf.
[Entering R.H. with a very red nose and tipsey.]
And all the wine's turned sour.
Barbara
[Looking ruefully at him.]
There's another comfort. To think that I, the right honorable foster-mother that was to have been, to her right honorable ladyship that was to have been ; for whom many a young and tender heart has sighed in vain, should ever have thrown herself away upon a wine butt.
Puf.
[Hic.]
A wine-butt, Madam Van Puffengruntz?
Bar.
Aye, a wine-butt--a beer barrel--a brute that hasn't drawn a sober breath since the day we were married, now more than three months ago.
Puf.
[Hic.]
A beer-barrel!
Bar.
[Savagely.]
I said a beer-barrel.
Von Puff
[Hic.]
Certainly ; of course, just which ever you please, my dear, wine butt or beer barrel, its all the same to me ; you know you will cackle.
Barbara
Cackle?
Puf.
[Hic.]
Cackle, my dear. You know I never have a moment's peace. You're not even quiet when you're asleep ; you snore, Madam Von Puffengrunz, you snore, loud enough to split the drum of my ear and rip up the seam up my night-cap.
Bar.
Snore! you wretch! I snore?
Puf.
[Hic.]
Yes, my dear, and have the night-mare. [Hic.] I don't like a wife that snores, and I hate a wife that has the night-mare. In future, I am going to have separate apartments. Hereafter, I intend to sleep alone.
Bar.
Alone?
Puf.
[Hic.]
I said alone.
Bar.
Am I awake? Who and what do you take me for?
Puf.
[Hic.]
I took you for a gentle spice--a sort of seasoning to the dull life I led here in the castle--but damn it, Madam, you've turned out to be all the condiments in one--a bottom layer of mustard, a top dressing of cayenne pepper, and a subeterranean lake of vinegar in the middle.
Bar.
[Enraged and approaching him--he retreats around L. H.]
Cackle! snore! nightmare! separate apartments! cayenne pepper! You wretch! You--you sot! you villain! I'll pepper you!
[Pulls his wig off.]
There! take that! and that! and that!
[Beats him over the poll with it 'till both off L.H.]
Curtain
