(Starting.)
I beg his infernal Majesty's pardon. I hope I have not said anything to offend; but strange sights and sounds are in the air. Birds that ought to have been to roost an hour ago fly screaming from tree to tree. The dismal screech-owl, from its hole in the oak, answers to the croak of the dreary monk-raven, and just now I saw a big fat buck scampering through the forest with his tail on fire. Oh dear, oh dear! what fearful omens! And I'm to be married to-morrow. If anything should happen to me! If my poor, dear, darling little Carline should happen to be left a widow before she's made a wife--if I should never taste--that is, if I should never know --Oh, Lord! it won't bear thinking of. I'll try once more.
