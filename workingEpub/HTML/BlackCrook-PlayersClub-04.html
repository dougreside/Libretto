Hertzog
(Rising in pain and with difficulty, and closing the book, he comes forward.)
Vain, vain! Some subtle spell is hovering in the air that mocks my power and makes the charms which once were potent a gibbering, idle sound. And shall I yield to this Invisible? I, Hertzog the Crook, whom men call Sorcerer. I, at whose name the strong man trembles and the weak grow faint. I, whose whole life of long, laborious years hath well nigh run its course, gleaning dark knowledge in forbidden paths-- shall I now seek the light?
